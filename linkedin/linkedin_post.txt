Validating R packages for regulatory submissions (GxP, FDA, EMA) is slow, manual, and repeated independently by hundreds of
 organizations worldwide. The same packages — dplyr, ggplot2, survival — validated over and over again. That's 100,000+ hours
  of duplicated industry effort every year.

 We think the R community can do better.

 That's why, at #WeData, we're working on valR — an open-source R package that automates the full validation pipeline:

 - Risk classification (LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH)
 - Automated IQ/OQ qualification with signed PDF reports
 - Auto-generated GxP documentation (URS, FS, IQ, OQ, PQ)
 - Reproducible environment validation capsules

 We're preparing a proposal for the R Consortium ISC Grant — and before we submit, we'd love to hear from you.

 Are you working in pharma, biotech, or clinical research and dealing with R package validation? Have you used riskmetric,
 valtools, or Axon.R? What's missing?

 Your feedback would mean a lot and help shape what we build.
