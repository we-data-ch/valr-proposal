Fabrice Hategekimana I work in pharma (100% R-based CRO; >400 packages used in the last 10 years) and I'm responsible for maintaining the validated R environment. But validation means something else to me:
1. "are the computed results valid numerically?"
2. "are the computed results compliant with SAS (the major de facto industry standard in pharma)? If not - why?" It doesn't mean that SAS is always right at all. Only that if the industry (using SAS in most submissions and non-submitted studies) asks me for the discrepancies, I am able to explain them clearly: a) error in R, b) both correct but R uses different settings/approach/parametrization/optimization. So I won't answer "I have no idea why our calculations differ".
I'm on the opinion that:
1. top popularity (thousands of downloads, 100s stars on GitHub, citations in in dozens of papers, tutorials, presentations, conferences)
2. rich resources (vignettes, tutorials, etc.)
3. top code quality (matching certain criteria)
don't matter at ll if the results are wrong so my study fails. I call it "validated-by-being-nice".
Therefore, I'm most interested in the numerical validations aspects.
